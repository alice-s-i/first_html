How to Make It:

start by chopping the onion and peeling the garlic. in my experience I found that chopping the garlic is not that crucial, throwing a couple of whole garlic cloves works just fine, you get the taste and smell you need without your hands smelling like garlic for the next two days. in a large pan, heat the olive oil with butter (i‚Äôm obsessed with this combo for no reason) over medium heat. add your onions and garlic. meanwhile, bring the pot of salted water to a boil. when onions are softened, add your tomatoes (if you use cherry tomatoes or regular ones, don‚Äôt forget to chop them a bit beforehand). while the sauce is cooking, add pasta to the hot salted water and cook it according to the instructions on the package until al dente. add fresh green basil along with all the spices to the tomato sauce and stir. you can also add a bit of pasta water to make the sauce less thick. add the cooked pasta to the sauce and mix well. leave it on the stove for a few minutes. serve it with insane amount of grated cheese
